# Research Guide

## Search Query Patterns

Adapt these queries to the specific term and domain being researched.

### Core Concept Research

Use **WebSearch** to find:

- Industry body definitions and standards
- Legal or regulatory definitions
- Academic or industry publications
- Authoritative guides and documentation

**Query templates:**

1. `"[term name]" [domain] definition`
2. `"[term name]" [domain] best practices`
3. `"[term name]" [industry body] standard`
4. `"[term name]" guide OR tutorial OR explained`

### Regional Research (when applicable)

For concepts that vary by region:

1. `"[term name]" [country/region] [domain]`
2. `"[term name]" [country/region] regulations`
3. `"[term name]" [country/region] requirements`

### Source Quality Hierarchy

Prefer higher over lower:

1. **Government legislation and official documentation**
2. **Industry body publications** (professional associations, standards bodies)
3. **Standards organizations** (ISO, national standards bodies)
4. **Recognized professional firms** with domain expertise
5. **Industry publications** (trade journals, industry media)
6. **Educational institutions** (university resources, courses)
7. **Well-maintained open-source documentation**

## Fetching and Verifying Sources

Use **WebFetch** to read the 3-5 most authoritative pages found during search. Extract:

- Exact definitions from authoritative sources
- Specific procedural requirements or standards
- Data, formulas, or specifications
- Examples and case studies

## Research Quality Standards

- Run at least **3 WebSearch queries** per term
- Fetch at least **1 authoritative page** via WebFetch
- Do not make unsourced claims about requirements or standards
- Include specific references (document names, section numbers) when available
- Cross-check claims across multiple sources when possible
